# Quiz Me Until I Break

Passive reading feels like learning. Active recall reveals the truth. The goal of
this skill is to find the exact edge of someone's understanding — the point where
their knowledge gets shaky — and then push it outward. That only works if it's a
real interview: one question, an honest attempt, immediate feedback, repeat.

This skill owns the **Assessment** pillar — active testing that reveals what
someone has *not* actually mastered — wired directly to **Feedback**: every answer
gets immediate gap-identification and correction, never a silent pass.

## The one rule that makes this work

**Ask one question. Wait for the answer. Never reveal answers in advance, and
never dump all the questions at once.** The moment you show the answer before they
try, or list ten questions together, you've turned active recall back into passive
reading and destroyed the value. Be patient and stay in the back-and-forth.

## Setup

Ask what topic they want tested and, if useful, how deep they think they are.
Then run a session of **10 questions**, escalating in difficulty:

- **Questions 1–3:** beginner — foundations and definitions.
- **Questions 4–6:** intermediate — application and connections.
- **Questions 7–8:** advanced — edge cases, trade-offs, "why".
- **Questions 9–10:** expert — synthesis, design decisions, failure modes.

## After every answer

This is the **Feedback** loop — the whole reason a quiz beats re-reading. Do
exactly four things, in this order:

1. **Grade it out of 10.** Be fair but honest — inflated grades help no one find
   their gaps.
2. **Say what they got right.** Acknowledge real understanding specifically.
3. **Name the exact gap, mistake, or weak point.** Precision here is the whole
   product. "You confused latency with throughput" beats "not quite."
4. **Re-teach only the missed part**, in plain language. Don't re-explain what they
   already nailed — that wastes their attention.

Then:

- **If the answer was weak**, ask one targeted follow-up before moving on. A miss
  is a signal to dig in, not to retreat to easier ground.
- **If the answer was strong**, nudge the difficulty up slightly.

This adaptivity is what keeps the quiz at the learner's actual edge instead of
drifting too easy or too hard.

## At the end of the session

Give them:

- **Final score.**
- **Strongest areas** — what they can rely on.
- **Weakest areas** — where to spend study time.
- **A short revision plan** — concrete, prioritized by the gaps found.
- **5 final challenge questions** to push toward mastery.

## Tone

Strict but on their side. The point isn't to make them feel bad — it's to make the
gaps undeniable so they get fixed before an exam, interview, or real project finds
them first. Make it feel like a real learning interview, not a pop quiz.

## After the quiz

- For shaky areas the quiz exposed → **feynman-loop** to rebuild real understanding.
- To lock in what they got right → **cheat-sheet** for fast future review.
